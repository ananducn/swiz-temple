Our purpose

Why build this temple?

✦

More than a building — a living center of faith, culture, and belonging for the Indian community and all friends of Vedic tradition in Switzerland.

✦

Spiritual Center

A consecrated sanctum for darshan, prayer, and reflection — the first traditional Krishna temple of its kind in Switzerland.

✦

Cultural Heritage

✦

✦

Youth Education

Gita classes, Sanskrit, values education, and mentorship that root the next generation in timeless wisdom.

✦

Festivals

✦

Daily Worship

Morning and evening aarti, bhajans, and prasadam — a steady rhythm of devotion, every single day.

The vision

Where Himalayan tradition meets the Alps

Designed in the classical Nagara style by traditional temple architects, the mandir will rise in harmony with its Alpine surroundings — hand-carved stone, a golden kalash crowning the shikhara, and gardens of lotus and native wildflowers.

Our mission is simple: to create a sanctuary of peace and devotion that serves every generation — a home for Lord Krishna, and for all who seek Him.

The divine inspiration

About Lord Krishna

Lord Krishna — the eighth avatar of Vishnu, divine teacher of the Bhagavad Gita, and embodiment of love, wisdom, and joy — has guided seekers for over five thousand years. His message of selfless action and devotion transcends every border.

"Whatever you do, whatever you eat, whatever you offer, whatever you give — do that as an offering unto Me."

— Bhagavad Gita 9.27

This temple is our collective offering — built brick by brick with devotion, so His presence may bless Switzerland for generations.

Questions

Frequently asked

Are donations tax-deductible?+

Yes. The Indian Association in Switzerland is a registered non-profit, and donations are tax-deductible in most cantons. You receive an official donation receipt for your tax declaration each January.

How is my donation used?+

100% of donations go to the temple project fund, held in a dedicated Swiss bank account. Finances are audited annually by an independent auditor and published in our yearly report to all members.

When will construction begin?+

Groundbreaking is planned for spring 2027, once fundraising reaches 85% of the goal. The building permit is already granted, so construction can start as soon as funding allows.

Can I donate from outside Switzerland?+

Absolutely. We accept international bank transfers and card payments in CHF, EUR, USD, and INR. Devotees from 14 countries have already contributed.

Will my sponsorship be recognised?+

Every sponsor is recorded in the temple's permanent donor book. Pillar and sanctum sponsors may dedicate their contribution in the name of a loved one, inscribed within the temple.

How can I volunteer?+

We welcome help with events, translation, communications, and professional expertise in construction, law, and finance. Write to us at seva@krishnamandir.ch and we'll find the right seva for you.
